Before Your Call

Common Questions

What is The Order of Kings?
A 6-month brotherhood and coaching experience for Christian entrepreneurs integrating faith, family, and business at the highest level. Not a course. Not a traditional mastermind. An apostolic framework built on Kingdom principles — designed to multiply the man so the mission multiplies with him.
Who is this call for?
High-capacity men who feel a gap between where they are and where God is calling them. Men who are building — businesses, families, ministries — but sense something is misaligned. If you're teachable, hungry, and ready to receive revelation, this conversation is for you.
Who will I be speaking with?
A member of Dan's team trained in the framework and vision behind The Order of Kings. Their job isn't to sell you — it's to understand where you are and whether this is the right environment for this season of your life.
What will we talk about on the call?
Come ready to be honest about where you are — in your business, your marriage, your walk with God. The call is a discernment conversation, not a pitch. You'll leave with clarity either way.
Should my spouse know I'm getting on this call?
Yes. Dan's framework starts with the marriage. If your wife has questions or reservations, she's welcome on the call. Some of the most important conversations happen when both spouses are in the room.
Will this help my marriage?
Yes — and this is one of the things that makes The Order of Kings distinct. Dan's framework treats marriage as the first domain of a man's Kingdom authority. A man winning at business but losing at home is not winning. The program brings health to the marriage as the business grows, not at its expense.
Is there support for raising my kids and leading my family?
Absolutely. The Father-First Framework is built into the program. Dan has seven children and works alongside his family — his sons lead organizations, his wife oversees a business. The model he teaches is the model he lives.
Is there real business education and coaching?
Yes. The program includes systems, frameworks, and coaching to help you stop being the bottleneck in your own business — building a team and structure that doesn't require your constant presence. Kingdom principles land in your actual operations.
Will this help me with my time?
This is one of the most common things men bring into the program. A core piece of the framework is what Dan calls the Time Redemption Strategy — a practical approach to reclaiming and stewarding your time as a Kingdom resource, not just managing a calendar.
How much time does this require from me?
The program is designed for men who don't have time to waste — the time ask is intentional and high-ROI. Exact commitments will be covered on your call, but the goal is always leverage: less of you in the wrong places, more of you where it counts.
What if I'm not sure I'm ready?
The men who aren't sure they're ready are often exactly the men this was built for. Readiness isn't about having everything together — it's about being done tolerating the gap between your current reality and your calling.
What should I do before the call?
Show up. Block the time, find a quiet place, and come with honesty about where you actually are — not where you wish you were. The more real you are, the more valuable the conversation will be.
What happens after the call?
If it's a fit, you'll be walked through what joining looks like and what the next steps are. If it's not the right time, you'll still leave with clarity. Either way, the conversation has value.
